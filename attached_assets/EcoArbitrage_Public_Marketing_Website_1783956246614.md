### Alternative tagline

### One-sentence description

EcoArbitrage helps climate funders connect project milestones, field evidence, external signals, uncertainty, and funding decisions in one auditable workflow.

### Short description

Climate projects generate reports, photos, financial records, surveys, and external data, but funders still struggle to determine what evidence supports each claim. EcoArbitrage turns this fragmented information into decision-ready milestone packets.

### Long description

EcoArbitrage is an early-stage evidence workflow for foundations and climate grant programs supporting locally led adaptation and nature projects.

Before a project milestone begins, the funder and implementing partner define what will be observed, what evidence is appropriate, who will collect it, and what limitations apply. During implementation, field submissions and approved external signals are connected to the claims they support. Reviewers can identify missing or contradictory evidence, record uncertainty, and prepare a transparent recommendation.

An authorized human makes the final funding decision.

EcoArbitrage does not sell offsets, certify environmental outcomes, or hold grant funds. It is designed to complement existing grant-management and finance systems.

### Primary value proposition

Make climate-grant milestone decisions with a clearer connection between claims, evidence, uncertainty, and authorized action.

### Supporting value propositions

#### Design evidence earlier

Define evidence requirements when the milestone is created, not after a report is questioned.

#### Reduce fragmented review

Bring field submissions, documents, external signals, and reviewer notes into one decision record.

#### Preserve uncertainty

Show what is supported, what is partial, what is missing, and what is not yet observable.

#### Support local usability

Use lightweight, mobile-first evidence requests designed to reduce duplicate reporting.

#### Build portfolio memory

Preserve what was expected, what was observed, and why a decision was made.

#### Complement existing systems

Import from and export to grant-management, finance, and document systems rather than replacing them.

### Objections and responses

#### “We already use a grant-management system.”

EcoArbitrage is not intended to replace it. Grant systems manage applications, awards, and reporting. EcoArbitrage focuses on the narrower workflow connecting a milestone to field evidence, corroborating signals, uncertainty, and the authorized decision.

#### “This sounds like an evaluator.”

The early service includes evidence-design support, but the aim is a continuous workflow rather than a one-time evaluation. Independent evaluators can also work through the system.

#### “Can satellite data verify our project?”

Not by itself. Satellite and other external data may corroborate selected environmental signals. They cannot independently prove causality, community outcomes, biodiversity improvement, or carbon additionality.

#### “Will grantees have to enter everything twice?”

The pilot should accept existing reports and evidence where possible. A core design requirement is reducing duplicate reporting rather than adding another layer.

#### “Does EcoArbitrage decide whether to release funds?”

No. It prepares the evidence and decision record. An authorized person at the funding organization decides.

#### “Does EcoArbitrage hold or transfer grant funds?”

No. The initial product does not provide payment, escrow, donation, or investment services.

#### “Is the product available now?”

EcoArbitrage is recruiting selected design partners. Capabilities shown on the website are conceptual or available only within agreed pilots unless stated otherwise.

### Section 17 — FAQ

#### Is EcoArbitrage a crowdfunding platform?

No. The initial product does not collect donations, offer investments, hold funds, or operate a public project marketplace.

#### Is EcoArbitrage a grant-management system?

No. It is intended to complement existing grant-management systems by focusing on milestone evidence and decision records.

#### Does EcoArbitrage verify carbon credits?

No. It does not issue, rate, certify, or sell carbon credits.

#### Can satellite imagery prove that a project succeeded?

No. Geospatial information can corroborate selected signals, such as land-cover or disturbance changes. It cannot independently prove causality, biodiversity outcomes, community benefit, or additionality.

#### Does AI make the funding decision?

No. Software may help organize and identify evidence, but an authorized human reviews and decides.

#### Will implementing partners need another complex system?

The pilot is being designed around lightweight evidence requests and reuse of existing materials. Reducing duplicate reporting is a core success criterion.

#### Who owns the project evidence?

Ownership, access, retention, and permitted use must be defined in the pilot agreement. EcoArbitrage should not reuse customer or community data beyond the agreed purpose.

#### Is the product generally available?

No. EcoArbitrage is currently intended for selected design-partner pilots.

#### Does EcoArbitrage guarantee project results?

No. Project outcomes depend on context, implementation, external conditions, and many factors beyond the software.

# `/methodology` — Methodology Page

## Hero

### Eyebrow

Evidence principles

### Headline

**A method designed to preserve what is known, unknown, and not yet observable**

### Body

Environmental and community projects produce different forms of evidence at different times. EcoArbitrage should not force all projects into one universal score.

The proposed method separates:

* claim;  
* observation;  
* source;  
* provenance;  
* corroboration;  
* interpretation;  
* uncertainty;  
* authorized decision.

## Inputs

Potential inputs include:

* grant agreement;  
* milestone plan;  
* theory of change;  
* budget;  
* prior report;  
* field submission;  
* measurement;  
* invoice;  
* survey;  
* consented photo or video;  
* community feedback;  
* project boundary;  
* external environmental signal;  
* financial-disbursement record.

## Process

### 1\. Identify the decision

What decision will the evidence inform?

### 2\. Identify the material claim

What must be true for the milestone to be approved?

### 3\. Define acceptable evidence

What evidence can reasonably support the claim?

### 4\. Define provenance

Who collects it, when, where, how, and under what incentives?

### 5\. Add context

What external conditions or signals matter?

### 6\. Review contradictions and gaps

What is absent, inconsistent, or too early to observe?

### 7\. Record uncertainty

What can and cannot be concluded?

### 8\. Preserve human decision authority

Who is authorized to approve, condition, hold, or escalate?

## Outputs

* Evidence plan.  
* Claim-evidence map.  
* Exception record.  
* Milestone packet.  
* Decision log.  
* Portfolio report.  
* Learning record.

## Measurement principles

### Relevance

Evidence should be relevant to the stated claim.

### Proportionality

Evidence burden should reflect grant size, risk, and decision significance.

### Triangulation

Material claims may require more than one source.

### Provenance

The origin and method of collection should remain visible.

### Timeliness

Evidence should be current enough for the decision.

### Uncertainty

Unknown or not-yet-observable results should not be forced into a positive or negative score.

### Participation

Implementing partners should help define feasible and respectful evidence.

### Non-substitution

Remote sensing and AI do not replace field knowledge or authorized human judgment.

## Trust model

Trust is built through layers:

* known source;  
* authenticated contributor;  
* provenance;  
* collection method;  
* consent;  
* conflict disclosure;  
* corroboration;  
* review;  
* decision accountability.

## Limitations

* Evidence may be incomplete.  
* Evidence may be manipulated.  
* External data may be too coarse.  
* Project effects may take years.  
* Causality may remain unresolved.  
* Community feedback may not be representative.  
* Funding decisions include value judgments outside the evidence.  
* A software record is not an independent assurance opinion.

## Ethical boundaries

* No covert tracking.  
* No unnecessary beneficiary identities.  
* No dangerous geolocation.  
* No automated grantee rejection.  
* No secondary use without authorization.  
* No unpaid expansion of reporting requirements.  
* No universal community ranking.  
* No direct donor access to individuals by default.

## Data-handling principles

* Collect the minimum necessary.  
* Define controller and processor roles.  
* Use agreed retention periods.  
* Support deletion and correction.  
* Encrypt data in transit and at rest.  
* Restrict access by role.  
* Record access and changes.  
* disclose subprocessors.  
* avoid sensitive data in public forms.  
* use appropriate safeguards for international transfers.

## O. Website Interactions

### Permitted

* Sticky navigation.  
* Smooth anchor scroll.  
* Accessible FAQ accordion.  
* Animated evidence-chain lines.  
* Hover reveal for limitations.  
* Product mockup tabs.  
* Before-and-after switch.  
* Scroll-triggered entrance.  
* Mobile navigation.  
* form loading, success, and error states.  
* simple evidence-status filtering within fictional mockups.  
* copy-to-clipboard for public company email.

### Avoid

* autoplay background video;  
* WebGL globe;  
* excessive parallax;  
* scroll hijacking;  
* cursor effects;  
* looping counters;  
* fake live data;  
* heavy carousel;  
* long page-load animation;  
* chatbot;  
* cookie banner unless required by selected analytics;  
* animation that hides content from screen readers.

### Reduced motion

When `prefers-reduced-motion` is enabled:

* remove line drawing;  
* remove scroll transitions;  
* show final state immediately;  
* retain normal tab and accordion behavior.
